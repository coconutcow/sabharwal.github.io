A journey around Mother Earth

My travels began as a light-hearted challenge: to experience 30 countries before I turn 30 years old. I completed this little challenge, and each new land, with its diverse culture and ethos, allowed me to immerse in perspectives vastly different from my own. It wasn’t just about ticking countries off a list, but about deepening my understanding of our shared humanity. Every destination revealed that, despite apparent differences, our dreams and struggles, joys and sorrows, are remarkably universal. The wisdom of ancient yogic sages echoed louder with every voyage— beneath the façade of myriad names (’naam’), forms (‘roop’), and behaviors (‘vyvhara’), the world resonates with a common heartbeat.

This expansive view equipped me to engage more empathetically in personal and professional environments. A broader worldview gave me the ability to appreciate diverse viewpoints, fostering collaboration and understanding in my people, students, friends, teams, and projects alike. I believe that borders are but lines on a map, and our shared dreams and aspirations are what truly connect us. Now, let me take you on a visual journey.
