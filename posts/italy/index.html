Italy

Sat Jan 01, 2022 · 273 words
life story

Italy was a pure accident. After having spent a good number of years working in the corporate space with some amazing minds, I had a strong sense of wanting to learn more and improve in some emerging areas such as machine learning. Before leaving a lucrative career, I wanted to be sure that my next jump would help me complete the objective of having a holistic understanding of technology and the underlying mathematics of these algorithms, frameworks, and methodologies.

I was hesitant to spend a large sum of money on educating myself, and instead began applying for scholarships in institutions that had a good faculty. To my surprise, I got accepted, with a “near” full scholarship (food, stay, tuition, all taken care of) to a program that had the same faculty teaching machine learning at MIT. I couldn’t say no, and had the opportunity to learn under some of the best minds in Machine Learning, Computer vision and Data science.

One thing lead to another, and this country grew on me. I came here to learn and eventually leave for other opportunities, but the country had me fall in love. I often say that Indians and Italians have three things in common, which are:

  1. Love for Food
  2. Love for Friends
  3. Love for Family

Gregory Roberts in his book ‘Shantaram’ very eloquently put it that Indians are the Italians of Asia, and Italians are the Indians of Europe - I couldn’t agree more. I thought I would only make acquaintances, but the land (made by its people) absorbed me, and I ended up with my own set of people, who I now call family.
